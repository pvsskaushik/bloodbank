Blood Bank

A blood bank is a center where blood gathered as a result of blood donation is stored and preserved for later use in blood transfusion.

About Blood Donation

Donating blood is a life saving measure especially when you have a rare blood type. Blood donation issafeand simple. It takes only about 10 minutes to donate blood - less than the time of an average telephonecall.We can save upto 3 to 4 precious lives by donating blood.

Universal Donors and Recipients

A universal donor is a person who can donate blood to any recipient of any blood group.

Universal blood donors have type O negative blood, which lacks A, B, and Rh antigens. This makes their blood compatible with all other blood types.

A universal blood recipient is a person with blood type AB, which can receive any type of blood during a transfusion, allowing them to receive any blood type without complications.

DO donate blood, only if you satisfy all of the following conditions

You are in good health and feeling well on the day of donation.
You are at least 17 years old
You weigh at least 50 kg (110 pounds).
You have had no tattoos or body piercings in the last 6-12 months
You have not donated blood within the last 8 weeks

DO NOT donate blood, if you have any of the following conditions

Cold / fever in the past 1 week.
If you have received a blood transfusion or an organ transplant in the past year.
Cardiac problems, hypertension, epilepsy, diabetes (on insulin therapy), history ofcancer, chronic kidney or liver disease, bleeding tendencies, venereal disease etc.
Major surgery in the last 6 months.
Vaccination in the last 24 hours.
Had fainting attacks during last donation.
Been tested positive for antibodies to HIV.
